    • How to use

    The Compound Interest Calculator helps you determine the future value of an investment based on the initial principal, the number of periods, and the rate of return. Follow the steps below to use the calculator:

    • Initial Value ($) : Enter the initial amount of money you are investing. For example, if you are starting with $100,000, input 100000 in this field.
    • Number of Times (Period) : Enter the number of periods for which the investment will grow. For instance, if you plan to invest for 5 years, input 5 in this field.
    • Rate of Return (%) : Input the annual rate of return as a percentage. For example, if you expect a 5% return per year, input 5 in this field.
    • Calculate : Click the green Calculate button to compute the compound interest. The calculator will process the inputs and display the results.
    • Result ($) : The Result field will show the final amount of your investment after the specified number of periods and the given rate of return. This value is automatically updated when you click Calculate.

    What is compound interest?

    Compound interest is the interest on a loan or deposit that is calculated based on both the initial principal and the accumulated interest from previous periods. Essentially, it means earning “interest on interest,” which can significantly increase the amount of interest earned or paid over time. This is a fundamental concept in finance and investing.

    Compound interest is a powerful tool in finance, helping investors grow their wealth over time and affecting how much borrowers pay on loans. Understanding how it works is essential for making informed financial decisions.

    Compound interest formula

    The compound interest formula is used to calculate the amount of interest earned on an investment or paid on a loan when interest is compounded over multiple periods. The formula considers the initial principal, the interest rate, the number of times interest is compounded per period, and the total number of periods.

    • A = the future value of the investment/loan, including interest
    • P = the principal investment/loan amount (the initial amount of money)
    • r = the annual interest rate (in decimal form, so 5% becomes 0.05)
    • n = the number of times interest is compounded per year
    • t = the number of years the money is invested or borrowed for

    Understanding the compound interest formula is crucial for making informed decisions about investments, savings, and loans, as it highlights how different variables affect the future value of money.

    What is rule of 4%?

    The 4% rule is a guideline used in retirement planning to estimate a sustainable withdrawal rate from a retirement portfolio. It suggests that retirees can withdraw 4% of their retirement savings in the first year of retirement and adjust that amount for inflation each subsequent year without running out of money over a 30-year retirement period.
