Compare Loan Types

A 30-year fixed-rate mortgage and a 15-year fixed-rate mortgage are two common types of home loans, each with distinct features. A 30-year fixed mortgage has a constant interest rate for the entire 30-year term, resulting in lower monthly payments compared to a 15-year fixed mortgage, but it typically comes with a higher interest rate and greater total interest paid over the life of the loan.

This type of loan is ideal for borrowers seeking lower monthly payments and planning to stay in their home for a long time. In contrast, a 15-year fixed mortgage also has a constant interest rate but for a shorter term of 15 years. It usually offers a lower interest rate, leading to higher monthly payments but significantly less total interest paid. This option is suitable for those who can afford the higher payments and wish to pay off their mortgage faster, thereby building home equity more quickly. The choice between these two depends on the borrower’s financial situation, long-term goals, and monthly budget.

How Your Mortgage Payment Is Calculated

Down Payment

A house down payment is the initial amount of money a buyer pays upfront when purchasing a home, typically expressed as a percentage of the home’s purchase price. This payment reduces the mortgage amount needed to finance the rest of the purchase. Larger down payments can lead to lower monthly mortgage payments, reduced interest over the life of the loan, and potentially avoiding private mortgage insurance (PMI), which is required if the down payment is less than 20%. Common down payment requirements vary by loan type, with conventional loans usually needing 5-20%, FHA loans requiring at least 3.5%, and VA and USDA loans often requiring no down payment. Planning and saving for a down payment is a crucial step in the home-buying process.

Homeowners Insurance

Home insurance, also known as homeowners insurance, is a type of property insurance that provides financial protection against damage or loss to an individual’s home and belongings. It typically covers various risks, including fire, theft, vandalism, and certain natural disasters. Home insurance also offers liability coverage, which protects homeowners if someone is injured on their property. Additionally, it may cover additional living expenses if the home becomes uninhabitable due to a covered event. Mortgage lenders usually require home insurance to protect their investment in the property. Overall, home insurance provides peace of mind by safeguarding one of the most significant investments a person can make.

Property Taxes

Property tax is a local tax imposed on real estate, calculated based on the assessed value of the property, including land and buildings. The tax rate is determined by local government authorities and is used to fund public services such as schools, police and fire departments, road maintenance, and other community infrastructure. Property owners are typically required to pay this tax annually or semi-annually, and the amount can vary significantly depending on the property’s location and value. Failure to pay property taxes can result in penalties, interest, and potentially foreclosure on the property.
